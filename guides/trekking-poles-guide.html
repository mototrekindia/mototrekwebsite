TREKKING POLES GUIDE

Trekking poles are commonly used by hikers to improve balance and reduce strain on the body. Here’s when they are useful and how beginners can use them effectively.

What Are Trekking Poles?

Trekking poles are lightweight walking sticks designed to provide extra support and balance while trekking on trails. They are commonly used by hikers and trekkers when walking on steep slopes, rocky paths, or long-distance routes.

At first glance, trekking poles may look simple, but they can make a big difference during a trek. By using your arms along with your legs, trekking poles help distribute effort across your entire body instead of placing all the strain on your knees and legs.

Many trekkers initially believe trekking poles are only necessary for very difficult mountain expeditions. However, they can be useful even on moderate treks, especially when dealing with long descents or slippery trails.

Once trekkers learn how to use them properly, trekking poles often become an essential part of their trekking gear.

Why Trekkers Use Trekking Poles

Trekking poles offer several practical benefits that make trekking safer and more comfortable, especially on longer or challenging trails.

  • They improve balance on uneven or rocky terrain
  • They reduce pressure on knees during steep descents
  • They help maintain a steady walking rhythm
  • They provide extra stability when crossing streams or slippery paths
  • They distribute effort between arms and legs, reducing fatigue

Many experienced trekkers particularly appreciate trekking poles when descending steep trails. Downhill walking can put significant pressure on the knees, and trekking poles help absorb some of that impact.

Trekking poles provide extra stability on uneven mountain trails.

Types of Trekking Poles

There are different types of trekking poles available, and choosing the right one often depends on the type of trek and personal preference.

  • Adjustable poles – These can be shortened or extended depending on terrain.
  • Foldable poles – Lightweight and compact, ideal for travel and backpacking.
  • Shock-absorbing poles – Designed to reduce impact during downhill trekking.
  • Fixed-length poles – Strong and simple but less flexible for changing terrain.

For most trekkers, adjustable trekking poles are the most versatile option because they allow you to change the pole length depending on whether you are climbing uphill or descending.

How to Use Trekking Poles Correctly

Using trekking poles properly makes them far more effective. Many beginners simply carry them without adjusting their technique, which reduces their benefits.

  • Adjust the pole length so your elbow forms a 90-degree angle
  • Plant the pole slightly ahead of your step for better balance
  • Use both poles to maintain a steady rhythm while walking
  • Shorten the poles slightly while climbing uphill
  • Lengthen them slightly during steep descents

Over time, using trekking poles becomes very natural. Many trekkers find that they help maintain a smooth and consistent pace on long trails.

When You Should Use Trekking Poles

Trekking poles are especially useful in certain situations. While they are not always necessary for very short or flat walks, they become extremely helpful in more demanding conditions.

Trekkers commonly use poles when hiking on steep mountain trails, long-distance treks, uneven rocky terrain, or during descents where knee strain is higher. They can also provide extra stability when crossing streams or slippery sections of the trail.

For beginners, trekking poles can offer additional confidence and balance, particularly on unfamiliar terrain. Many people who start using them quickly realize how helpful they are during longer treks.

Even experienced trekkers often carry poles as part of their regular trekking gear because of the stability and comfort they provide.

Mototrek Tip

If you're planning a long trek or a route with steep descents, trekking poles can significantly reduce strain on your knees and improve stability. For beginners especially, they can make challenging trails feel much more manageable.
